    Vogueon

    Oh fearless poetry magazine,
    Thy brightness is to us,
    As glorious gabbleblotchits, in mid-winter evenings,
    On a luscious tree,
    That heavily has fallen down,
    Its roots jurtles, grumbling,
    Into a viable festering of papery interior screams.
    Now the glorious poets manufacturings,
    Are proclaiming noisily up the galaxies,
    And living amateurs fraternize and spout,
    Like mouthing muffled carcasses,
    Groop, we thank you, foonting turlingdromes,
    taht hooptiously drangle us,
    With crinkly configurations, lyric make-ups.
    Or else we shall rend thee in the oblivion with our lesser,
    See if we don't!
