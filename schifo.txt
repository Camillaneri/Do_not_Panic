<p class="left"><span id="L2" class="mention place" about="Earth" data-label="Earth" data-wikidata-id="Q2">Planet Earth</span> is home to some <span id="C7" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Amazement">spectacular relics from bygone eras</span>, constructions that seem to <span id="C8" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Amazement">defy the technological capabilities of their time either because they’re too big, too heavy, or too complex</span>.</p>

<p>As such, some suggest the <span id="C9" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Alien Help"><span id="C10" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Constructor">ancient builders</span> of the <span id="L3" class="mention place" about="Pyramids" data-label="Pyramids" data-wikidata-id="Q175582">Egyptian pyramids</span>, the <span id="L4" class="mention place" about="NazcaLines" data-label="Nazca Lines" data-wikidata-id="Q2620036">Nasca lines</span>, and others were following an extraterrestrial instruction manual</span>. Perhaps the <span id="C11" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Alien">hands that <span id="C12" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Craft">crafted these sites</span> weren’t really of this world</span>.</p>

<p>To be sure, it’s fun to think about <span id="C13" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Alien Help">whether aliens have visited <span id="L5" class="mention place" about="Earth" data-label="Earth" data-wikidata-id="Q2">Earth</span></span>. After all, <span id="C13" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Human">humans</span> are on the threshold of <span id="C14" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Explore">expanding our reach in space</span>, and places like <span id="L6" class="mention place" about="Mars" data-label="Mars" data-wikidata-id="Q111">Mars</span> are in our sight. But the truth is,<span id="C15" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Human Creation">there’s no evidence suggesting that <span id="C16" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Alien">aliens</span> have ever been here</span>. And invoking a <span id="C17" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Supernatural">supernatural explanation</span> for some of the <span id="C18" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Amazement">most monumental of <span id="C19" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Human">human</span> achievements</span> means skipping over the <span id="C19" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Amazement">fascinating ways in which <span id="C20" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Primitives">prehistoric civilizations</span> managed to make some of the <span id="C21" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Amazement">largest and most enigmatic constructions</span> on <span id="L7" class="mention place" about="Earth" data-label="Earth" data-wikidata-id="Q2">Earth</span></span>.</p>


<h2 class="midSmall distant"><span id="L8" class="mention place" about="Sacsayhuamán" data-label="Sacsayhuamán" data-wikidata-id="Q828336">Sacsayhuamán</span></h2>


<figure class="center close">
    <img class="expand" src="Alien_1.jpg" alt="The ancient fortress of Sacsayhuamán contrasts with Cusco's modern buildings." />
    <figcaption class="captionPhoto"><p>The <span id="C22" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">ancient fortress</span> of <span id="L9" class="mention place" about="Sacsayhuamán" data-label="Sacsayhuamán" data-wikidata-id="Q828336">Sacsayhuamán</span> contrasts with <span id="L10" class="mention place" about="Cusco" data-label="Cusco" data-wikidata-id="Q205057">Cusco</span>'s <span id="C23" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Building">modern buildings</span>.</p>

<hr class="small left yellow">
<br>

<p>Outside the old <span id="L11" class="mention place" about="Cusco" data-label="Cusco" data-wikidata-id="Q205057">Inca capital of Cusco</span>, a <span id="C24" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">fortress called <span id="L12" class="mention place" about="Sacsayhuamán" data-label="Sacsayhuamán" data-wikidata-id="Q828336">Sacsayhuamán</span></span> rests in the <span id="L13" class="mention place" about="Andes" data-label="Andes" data-wikidata-id="Q5456">Peruvian Andes</span>. Built from <span id="C25" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Material">enormous stones that have been chiseled and stacked together like a jigsaw puzzle</span>, some say <span id="C26" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Alien Help"><span id="L14" class="mention place" about="Sacsayhuamán" data-label="Sacsayhuamán" data-wikidata-id="Q828336">Sacsayhuamán</span> could be the work of an <span id="C27" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Primitives">ancient civilization</span> that had a little help from <span id="C28" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Alien">interstellar friends</span></span>.</p>

<p>The <span id="C29" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument"><span id="C30" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">1,000-year-old</span> interlocking fortress walls</span> are made of <span id="C31" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Material">rocks that weigh as much as 360 tons each</span>, and which were <span id="C32" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Process">carried more than 20 miles before being lifted and fit into place with laser-like precision</span>.</p>

<p>How an <span id="C33" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Primitives">ancient culture</span> accomplished such a <span id="C34" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Precision">feat of engineering</span> is a fun little problem to solve; <span id="C35" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Historical Reconstruction">turns out the <span id="C36" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Inca</span> were as adept at building houses and fortified complexes as they were at watching the sky and keeping calendars</span>. In fact, <span id="L15" class="mention place" about="Sacsayhuamán" data-label="Sacsayhuamán" data-wikidata-id="Q828336">Sacsayhuamán</span> isn’t the only example of this intricate masonry: <span id="C37" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">Similar walls</span> exist throughout the <span id="L15" class="mention place" about="IncaEmpire" data-label="Inca Empire" data-wikidata-id="Q28573">Inca Empire</span>, including one in <span id="L16" class="mention place" about="Cusco" data-label="Cusco" data-wikidata-id="Q205057">Cusco</span> where a <span id="C38" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Process"><span id="C39" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Statue">12-angled stone</span> has been carefully wedged into place</span>.</p>

<p>More recently, <span id="C40" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Scientist">archaeologists</span> have uncovered <span id="C41" class="mention concept" about="interests" data-label="Archaeology">traces of the <span id="C42" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resource">rope-and-lever system the <span id="C43" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Inca</span> used to <span id="C44" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Process">transport stones from their quarries to their citie</span> </span>—a system that relied on <span id="C45" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resource">strength and ingenuity</span>, rather than <span id="C46" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Alien">alien architects</span></span>.</p>

<h2 class="midSmall distant"><span id="L17" class="mention place" about="NazcaLines" data-label="Nazca Lines" data-wikidata-id="Q2620036">Nasca lines</span></h2>

<figure class="center close">
    <img class="expand" src="Alien_2.jpg" alt="The ancient fortress of Sacsayhuamán contrasts with Cusco's modern buildings." />
    <figcaption class="captionPhoto"><p>A plane glides over an <span id="C47" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Trace">ancient spider geoglyph</span> in the <span id="L18" class="mention place" about="Desert" data-label="Peruvian desert" data-wikidata-id="Q8514">Peruvian desert</span>.</p>
    <p class="namePhoto close">PHOTOGRAPH BY <span id="P4" class="mention person" about="RobertClark" data-label="Robert Clark">ROBERT CLARK</span>, <span class="credit"><span id="O3" class="mention organization" about="NationalGeographic" data-label="National Geographic">NAT GEO IMAGE COLLECTION</span></span></p>

<hr class="small left yellow">
<br>

<p>On a <span id="L19" class="mention place" about="Desert" data-label="Peruvian desert" data-wikidata-id="Q8514">high and dry plateau some 200 miles southeast of <span id="L20" class="mention place" about="Lima" data-label="Lima" data-wikidata-id="Q2868">Lima</span></span>, more than <span id="C48" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Trace">800 long, straight white lines are etched into the <span id="L21" class="mention place" about="Desert" data-label="Peruvian desert" data-wikidata-id="Q8514">Peruvian desert</span>, seemingly at random. Joining them are <span id="C49" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Figure">300 geometric shapes and 70 figures of animals, including a spider, monkey, and hummingbird</span></span>.</p>

<p><span id="C50" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Trace">The longest of the lines run straight as an arrow for miles</span>. <span id="C51" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Figure">The biggest shapes stretch nearly 1,200 feet across and are best viewed from the air</span>. <span id="C52" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction"><span id="C53" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Scientist">Scientists</span> suspect the <span id="L22" class="mention place" about="NazcaLines" data-label="Nazca Lines" data-wikidata-id="Q2620036">Nasca drawings</span> are as many as <span id="C54" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">two millennia old</span></span>, and <span id="C55" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="">because of their age, size, visibility from above, and mysterious nature, <span id="L23" class="mention place" about="NazcaLines" data-label="Nazca Lines" data-wikidata-id="Q2620036">the lines</span> are often cited as one of the best examples of <span id="C56" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Alien Help">alien handiwork</span> on <span id="L24" class="mention place" about="Earth" data-label="Earth" data-wikidata-id="Q2">Earth</span>. Otherwise, how would an <span id="C57" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Primitives">ancient culture</span> have been able to make such <span id="C58" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Figure">huge designs in the desert</span> without <span id="C59" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resource">being able to fly</span>? And why?</span></p>

<p>Turns out, it’s rather easy to understand the how. Called <span id="C60" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Trace">geoglyphs, these enigmatic designs</span> are made by <span id="C61" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Process">removing the top, rust-colored layer of rocks and exposing the brighter white sand underneath</span>.</p>

<p>The why is a bit tougher to comprehend. <span id="C62" class="mention concept" about="interests" data-label="Antrophology">First studied in the early <span id="D2" class="mention date" about="'900" data-label="'900">1900s</span>, <span id="C63" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">the <span id="C64" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Figure">designs were initially suspected to be aligned with constellations or solstices</span>, but more recent work <span id="C65" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Historical Reconstruction">suggests the <span id="L25" class="mention place" about="NazcaLines" data-label="Nazca Lines" data-wikidata-id="Q2620036">Nasca lines</span> point to ceremonial or ritual sites related to water and fertility</span></span>. And in addition to being visible from the air, the shapes can be seen from surrounding foothills.</p>

<p>Just outside <span id="L30" class="mention place" about="Cairo" data-label="Cairo" data-wikidata-id="Q85">Cairo</span>, in <span id="L31" class="mention place" about="Giza" data-label="Giza" data-wikidata-id="Q81788">Giza</span>, the <span id="L32" class="mention place" about="GizaPyramids" data-label="Giza Pyramids" data-wikidata-id="Q13217298">most famous of Egypt’s pyramids</span> rise from the desert. Built more than <span id="C66" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">4,500 years ago</span>, the <span id="L33" class="mention place" about="GizaPyramids" data-label="Giza Pyramids" data-wikidata-id="Q13217298">Pyramids at Giza</span> are <span id="C67" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Historical Reconstruction"><span id="C68" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Monument">monumental tombs</span> where ancient queens and pharaohs were buried</span>.</p>

<p>But how, exactly, did the <span id="C69" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Egyptians</span> build these things? The <span id="L34" class="mention place" about="GizaPyramid" data-label="Great Giza Pyramid" data-wikidata-id="Q37200">Great Pyramid</span> is made of <span id="C70" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Material">millions of precisely hewn stones weighing at least two tons each</span>. Even with today’s cranes and other construction equipment, building a pyramid as big as that of <span id="P6" class="mention person" about="PharaohKhufu" data-label="Pharaoh Khufu">Pharaoh Khufu</span> would be a formidable challenge.</p>

<p>And then <span id="C71" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">there’s the astronomical configuration of <span id="L35" class="mention place" about="GizaPyramids" data-label="Giza Pyramids" data-wikidata-id="Q13217298">the pyramids</span>, which is said to align with the stars in <span id="L36" class="mention place" about="OrionBelt" data-label="Orion BeltOrion" data-wikidata-id="Q8860">Orion’s belt</span>. As well, <span id="C72" class="mention concept" about="interests" data-label="Alien Theories">alien theorists often point to the fact that <span id="C72" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Alien Help">these <span id="L37" class="mention place" about="GizaPyramids" data-label="Giza Pyramids" data-wikidata-id="Q13217298">the pyramids</span>three pyramids</span> are in way better shape than others built centuries later (never mind the amount of work that has gone into preserving them over the past several centuries)</span></span>.</p>

<p>So are <span id="L38" class="mention place" about="Egypt" data-label="Egypt" data-wikidata-id="Q79">Egypt</span>’s pyramids artifacts of <span id="C73" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Alien">aliens</span>? Not exactly. It’s true that <span id="C74" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction"><span id="C75" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Scientist">scientists</span> aren’t quite sure how the <span id="C76" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Popyulation">ancient Egyptian</span> built the pyramids—and especially how they did it so quickly—but there’s ample evidence that <span id="C77" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">these tombs</span> are the work of <span id="C78" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resource">thousands of earthly hands</span></span>.</p>

<p>A <span id="C79" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">huge circle of stones, some weighing as much as 50 tons</span>, sits in the <span id="L41" class="mention place" about="Stonehenge" data-label="Stonehenge" data-wikidata-id="Q39671">English countryside outside Salisbury</span>. Known as <span id="L42" class="mention place" about="Stonehenge" data-label="Stonehenge" data-wikidata-id="Q39671">Stonehenge</span>, the <span id="C80" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">Neolithic monument</span> inspired Swiss author <span id="P8" class="mention person" about="ErichVonDaniken" data-label="Erich von Däniken">Erich von Däniken</span> to suggest <span id="C81" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">it was a model of the solar system that <span id="C82" class="mention concept" about="interests" data-label="Alien Theories">also functioned as an alien landing pad</span>—after all, how else could those massive stones have ended up hundreds of miles from their home quarry</span>?</p>

<p>No one knows what, exactly, the meaning of <span id="L43" class="mention place" about="Stonehenge" data-label="Stonehenge" data-wikidata-id="Q39671">Stonehenge</span> is, but, as with all the other sites in this collection, the explanation is not aliens. Instead, <span id="C83" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">scientists have demonstrated it’s actually possible to build such a thing using <span id="C84" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resources">technologies that would have been around <span id="C85" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">5,000 years ago</span>, when the earliest structures at the site were built</span>.</p>

<p>And now, <span id="C86" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">it appears as though the stones are aligned with solstices and eclipses, suggesting the <span id="C87" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Primitives">Stonehenge builders</span> were at least keeping an eye on the heavens, even if they didn’t come from above</span>.</p>

<p><span id="L47" class="mention place" about="Teotihuacán" data-label="Teotihuacán" data-wikidata-id="Q172613">Teotihuacán</span>, meaning the "City of the Gods," is a sprawling, ancient city in <span id="L48" class="mention place" about="Mexico" data-label="Mexico" data-wikidata-id="Q96">Mexico</span> that’s best known for its <span id="C88" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Monument">pyramidal temples</span> and <span id="C89" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">astronomical alignments</span>. Built <span id="C90" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">more than 2,000 years ago</span>, <span id="L49" class="mention place" about="Teotihuacán" data-label="Teotihuacán" data-wikidata-id="Q172613">Teotihuacán</span>’s age, size, and complexity can make it seem otherworldly, but it’s <span id="C91" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Human Creation">very much the work of humans</span>.</p>

<p>Scientists suspect that <span id="C92" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Historical Reconstruction">over centuries, a mix of cultures including <span id="C93" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Maya</span>, <span id="C94" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Zapotec</span>, and <span id="C95" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Mixtec</span> built the <span id="L50" class="mention place" about="Teotihuacán" data-label="Teotihuacán" data-wikidata-id="Q172613">city that could house more than 100,000 people</span></span>. With its <span id="C96" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Resource">murals, tools, transportation system, and evidence of advanced agricultural practices</span>, <span id="L51" class="mention place" about="Teotihuacán" data-label="Teotihuacán" data-wikidata-id="Q172613">Teotihuacán</span> is often considered <span id="C97" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Alien Help">much more technologically developed than should have been possible in pre-Aztec <span id="L52" class="mention place" about="Mexico" data-label="Mexico" data-wikidata-id="Q96">Mexico</span></span>.</p>

<p>By far, the most well known of <span id="L53" class="mention place" about="Teotihuacán" data-label="Teotihuacán" data-wikidata-id="Q172613">Teotihuacán</span>’s buildings is the massive <span id="L54" class="mention place" about="PyramidOfTheSun" data-label="Pyramid Of The Sun" data-wikidata-id="Q29238">Pyramid of the Sun</span>. One of the largest such constructions in the <span id="L55" class="mention place" about="WesternHemisphere" data-label="Western Hemisphere" data-wikidata-id="Q181982">Western Hemisphere</span>, <span id="C98" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">the pyramid’s curious alignment is believed to be based on calendrical cycles</span>.</p>

<p>The enigmas surrounding the <span id="C100" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="Statue">moai</span>, <span id="L58" class="mention place" about="EasterIsland" data-label="Easter Island" data-wikidata-id="Q14452">Easter Island</span>’s fleet of large stone figures</span>, pretty much follow the same narrative as the other sites described here: How in the world did the <span id="C101" class="mention concept" about="agents" data-label="Population">Rapa Nui</span> make these figures more than <span id="C102" class="mention concept" about="characteristics" data-label="Ancient">1,000 years ago</span>? <p><span id="C104" class="mention concept" about="tools" data-label="Material">Carved from stone</span>, the <span id="C105" class="mention concept" about="sights" data-label="">nearly 900 human figures are sprinkled along the flanks of the island’s extinct volcanoes</span>. <p>If <span id="P11" class="mention person" about="ElonMusk" data-label="Elon Musk">Elon Musk</span> has his way, humans will be capable of visiting the “<span id="L63" class="mention place" about="Mars" data-label="Mars" data-wikidata-id="Q58969">face on Mars</span>” sometime this century. Turns out that <span id="C121" class="mention concept" about="stories" data-label="Scientifical Reconstruction">what had appeared to be a face is just another boring old Martian mesa</span>, kind of like the landforms that litter the <span id="L65" class="mention place" about="US" data-label="US" data-wikidata-id="Q30">U.S. Southwest</span>.</p>

<p class="italic">But that doesn’t mean it wouldn’t be <span id="C122" class="mention concept" about="interests" data-label="Leisure">fun to visit</span>.</p>
